# The Sun Also Rises

Ernest Hemingway

---

# Genres

- Modernist Literature
- Literary Fiction


---

# Themes

- The Lost Generation
- Disillusionment
- Masculinity
- Nature and Ritual
- Unrequited Love


---

# Summary (1/3)

The story follows Jake Barnes, an American journalist living in 1920s Paris, and a group of expatriates as they navigate a life of heavy drinking and aimless socializing. Jake, physically and emotionally scarred by World War I, harbors a painful and unconsummated love for Lady Brett Ashley, an independent but deeply restless socialite.

---

# Summary (2/3)

Seeking escape, the group travels to Pamplona, Spain, for the festival of San Fermín. Against the backdrop of the running of the bulls and intense bullfighting, tensions between the men erupt over their shared fascination with Brett. The contrast between the stoic, traditional ritual of the bullring and the moral decay of the expatriates highlights their profound sense of loss.

---

# Summary (3/3)

Ultimately, the novel portrays the Lost Generation—individuals disillusioned by the horrors of war, struggling to find meaning or connection in a world that feels increasingly hollow. Despite their fleeting moments of passion and travel, they remain trapped in a cycle of dissatisfaction and unfulfilled desire.

---

# Key Points

- Employs the Iceberg Theory of writing, where the true emotional depth is left unspoken
- The bullfight serves as a metaphor for purity and stoic suffering in contrast to the characters' messy lives
- Explores the psychological and physical wounds of World War I
- Highlights the shifting social and sexual dynamics of the 1920s
