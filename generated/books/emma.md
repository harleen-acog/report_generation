## Summary

A precocious and wealthy young woman in the village of Highbury, Emma Woodhouse, occupies herself with misguided matchmaking for her friends. Through a series of social blunders and romantic misunderstandings, she is forced to confront her own vanity and the reality of her feelings for her longtime mentor, Mr. Knightley.


## Key Points

- Features an 'anti-heroine' whom Austen famously described as someone no one but herself would much like
- Masterfully employs free indirect discourse to immerse the reader in the protagonist's subjective perspective
- Explores the restricted agency of women in the 19th-century landed gentry
- Satirizes the rigid social structures and etiquette of provincial English life
